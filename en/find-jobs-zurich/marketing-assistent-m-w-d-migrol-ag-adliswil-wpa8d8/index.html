Role overview

Are you an organizational talent and enjoy operational work? In this versatile position, you are responsible for cross-divisional POP communication, coordinate the support of our digital customer feedback channels and support the team in administrative and operational tasks. An exciting task awaits you, in which you can actively contribute to further development!

Mansioni

  • Independent organization and implementation of communication measures at the point of purchase (POP) for all business areas, but with a focus on filling station business
  • Responsibility for ordering, managing and coordinating POP materials for the filling station business
  • Community management for digital customer feedback channels including answering standard concerns and forwarding more complex topics to the responsible internal bodies
  • Administrative and operational support of the marketing teams for the areas and the head of overall marketing

Requirements

  • At least 3 years of professional experience: operational marketing/communication
  • Completed vocational apprenticeship (EFZ): KV with further training in marketing
  • Good knowledge of Adobe InDesign and digital tools for project coordination and communication
  • Experience in coordinating and implementing communication measures, ideally for POP communication
  • High communication skills and pleasure in working with different teams and partners
  • Strong organisational skills and a structured way of working
  • in German (negotiation certain)

Cosa of Friamo

  • Flexible place of work / home office: possibility to work in the home office on 1 to 2 days per week
  • Occupational pension scheme: Migros pension fund. Breakdown of premiums: Migrol 2/3, employee 1/3
  • Social benefits: payment of capital to survivors in the event of death
  • Employee offers: gifts on special occasions such as wedding, birth or retirement
  • Personal responsibility & freedom: Promoting personal responsibility in the professional environment
  • Flat hierarchies: Short decision paths thanks to our flat hierarchical levels
