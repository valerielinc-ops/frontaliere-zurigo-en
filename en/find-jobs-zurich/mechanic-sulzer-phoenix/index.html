Main responsibilities

  • Essential Duties and Responsibilities
  • Disassemble, inspect, repair, test, and reassemble mission-critical industrial motors, generators, pumps, gearboxes, and related electromechanical equipment used in heavy industry.
  • Work on high-value rotating equipment serving power, water, mining, manufacturing, semiconductor, aerospace, agriculture, food production, and other industrial markets.
  • Inspect and evaluate equipment condition, damage, failure evidence, repair requirements, and serviceability.
  • Perform low-voltage and high-voltage electrical diagnostic testing and related electrical inspections on motors and generators.
  • Troubleshoot mechanical and electromechanical problems using test results, inspection findings, drawings, repair history, and hands-on experience.
  • Remove, install, fit, adjust, and repair mechanical components during disassembly, reassembly, shop repair, and on-site service.
  • Inspect mechanical and electrical components for wear, damage, overheating, tracking, contamination, fit issues, and other repair concerns.
  • Measure, verify, and record critical dimensions using micrometers, calipers, dial indicators, bore gauges, and other precision measuring tools.
  • Identify, match-mark, organize, protect, clean, and document parts during teardown and repair.
